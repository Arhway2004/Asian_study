Overview of Taiwan’s Population

Taiwan has a population of approximately **23 million** people, making it one of the most densely populated places in the world. The majority of the population resides in urban areas, with Taipei, Kaohsiung, and Taichung being the largest cities.

Demographics and Ethnic Composition

1. Ethnic Groups

The majority of Taiwan’s population (around 97%) consists of Han Chinese, mainly Hoklo and Hakka. Indigenous groups, such as the Amis and Atayal, make up about 2.5% of the population.

2. Population Density

Taiwan has a high population density, averaging around **650 people per square kilometer**. The western plains are more populated than the mountainous east.

3. Aging Population

With a declining birth rate and increased life expectancy, Taiwan is experiencing rapid aging. Over **16% of the population** is aged 65 and above.

4. Urban vs Rural Population

Nearly **80% of Taiwanese people** live in urban areas. Taipei, the capital, is the most densely populated city.

5. Birth Rate and Migration Trends

Taiwan has one of the world's lowest birth rates, leading to government initiatives to encourage family growth and attract foreign talent.
