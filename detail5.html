Cultural Festivals of Taiwan

Discover the vibrant festivals that showcase Taiwan’s rich heritage and traditions.

Overview

Taiwan is home to a diverse range of cultural festivals that reflect its deep-rooted traditions, spiritual beliefs, and modern creativity. From colorful lantern displays to lively folk celebrations, these festivals attract both locals and international visitors, offering a glimpse into Taiwan’s unique cultural identity.

Taiwan's Cultural Festivals

Experience the vibrant cultural festivals of Taiwan, from the mesmerizing Lantern Festival to the thrilling Dragon Boat races.

Major Cultural Festivals in Taiwan

1. Lunar New Year (Chinese New Year)

The most important festival in Taiwan, Lunar New Year marks the start of the traditional Chinese calendar. Families gather for reunion dinners, give red envelopes (*hongbao*), and celebrate with fireworks and dragon dances. Temples hold special ceremonies to bring luck for the year ahead.

2. Lantern Festival

Held on the 15th day of the Lunar New Year, the Lantern Festival is famous for the sky lantern release in Pingxi. People write their wishes on lanterns and release them into the night sky, creating a magical scene. The festival also features lantern displays, traditional performances, and street food.

3. Dragon Boat Festival

Celebrated in honor of the ancient poet Qu Yuan, the Dragon Boat Festival features exciting dragon boat races held in rivers and lakes across Taiwan. Families also eat *zongzi* (sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves) as part of the tradition.

4. Ghost Festival

Observed in the seventh month of the lunar calendar, the Ghost Festival is a time when spirits of the deceased are believed to return to the living world. Families make food offerings, burn incense, and release floating lanterns on rivers to guide spirits back peacefully.

5. Mid-Autumn Festival

Also known as the Moon Festival, this event celebrates the harvest season. Families gather to admire the full moon, eat mooncakes, and hold barbecues. The festival is also famous for its tradition of pomelo fruit carving.

6. Double Ten Day (Taiwan National Day)

Held on October 10th, Double Ten Day is Taiwan’s national holiday, celebrating the founding of the Republic of China. The day features grand military parades, fireworks, and various cultural performances.

7. Mazu Pilgrimage

One of the largest religious processions in Taiwan, the Mazu Pilgrimage honors the sea goddess Mazu. Thousands of devotees participate in a multi-day pilgrimage, walking across different cities and stopping at temples along the way.

Why Taiwan’s Festivals Matter

Festivals in Taiwan are more than just celebrations; they are a way for people to connect with history, honor traditions, and strengthen social bonds. These events attract millions of tourists and help preserve the island’s unique cultural heritage.
